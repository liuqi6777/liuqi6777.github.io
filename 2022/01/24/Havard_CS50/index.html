Review for Harvard CS50’s Introduction to AI with Python

2022-01-24

Why to wirte this post

About two months ago, I found a website (csdiy.wiki) by accident, where a computer science major at Peking University recorded his self-learning process. There, he recorded many of the lessons he had studied and put the resources together.

Looking back on the study of the past semester, in fact, most of the time I was learning by myself. The discovery of this site gave me a bold idea. CS50’s Introduction to Artificial Intelligence with Python was the first course in which I completely finished by self-learning and tyied to complete the course projects.

But with only input and no output, it still feels like something is missing. Therefore, I decided to record my learning process in the form of a blog.

About the course

This course is a basic introduction to Artificial Intelligence, with a step-by-step introduction to some traditional AI algorithms, including search, logic, etc. This course doesn’t have a very deep mathematical derivation, it just gives you an intuitive sense of what you know in a very intelligible way. The highlight of this course is its 12 projects that use the knowledge to implement game AI, such as Tic-Tac-toe and minesweeper.

As an introductory course, I think it is absolutely excellent. Some traditional artificial intelligence knowledge taught in this course is a good supplement to the introduction to Artificial Intelligence course I took last semester.

Next, I will read the book Artificial Intelligence: A Modern Approach. This course certainly got me off to a good start.
